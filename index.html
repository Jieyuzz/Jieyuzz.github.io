From WHO

Misconceptions About Suicide

People who commit suicide are weak or crazy.

NO. Never judge someone like this. You are not them. You don’t know what they are going through.

People who talk about suicide won’t really do it.

NO. When someone is talking about suicide, it is not only a wanring, but also an attempt to seek help. Please don’t ignore them.

Risk Factors

  • Mental disorders, particularly mood disorders, schizophrenia, anxiety disorders, and certain personality disorders
  • History of trauma or abuse
  • Hopelessness
  • Family history of suicide
  • Previous suicide attempt(s)
  • Job or financial loss
  • Lack of social support and sense of isolation

From Suicide Prevention Lifeline

Warning Signs

  • Talking about wanting to die or to kill themselves
  • Talking about feeling hopeless or having no reason to live
  • Extreme mood swings
  • Sleeping too little or too much
  • Increasing the use of alcohol or drugs
  • Looking for a way to kill themselves
  • Showing rage or talking about seeking revenge
  • Withdrawing or isolating themselves

From Suicide Prevention Lifeline

What You Can Do

  • Tell the person that you are available to listen to them
  • Ask the person how you can help
  • Avoid giving advice. Trying to rush through fixing the situation can make it seem like you’re not available to listen
  • Don’t force them to talk. Let them speak whenever they are ready
  • Encourage the person to reach out for help
